A Stanley Tale

Insulation test of Stanley Pint Cup with cap.

The Test

It will be capture temperature of three sources:

  • The Stanley Cup itself, with cap;
  • Another cup, as "control group";
  • Temperature of the envirioment;

The cups will be filled with ice and water. It will be logged every 'n' seconds the the temperature of all sources, together with test time (time from the beginning of the test). The test finishes with 24h or if all the three sources stabelize close enough (how close?).

Parameters

  • Data will be logged every 2 seconds;
  • The cups composition are:
    • Control cup: made of aluminium / 79g / 750ml;
    • Staley cup: complex / 358g (302g without cap / nominally 0.8lb = 362g) / 600ml (nominally 16oz = 473ml);
  • Both cups were filled with: 25 cubes of ice and 200ml of water. After all ice melted, it was mesasured:
    • 403ml at Stanley Cup;
    • 400ml at control cup;

Sensor

It was used the DS18B20 temperature sensor with 12bit precision (± 0.5 ℃). The sensors weren't calibrated.

Data processing

  • If in a same instant, any sensor have a invalid read, all data will be dropped;
  • Data will be collapsed every 20 consecutive successful reads, keeping the last read time.
